SMAD 408: Independent Project Proposal

Gravity Explained:
An Interactive Animated Instructional Video/Experience

Overview

This idea started as an research project for the Astronomy department here at JMU. The concept was an educational video with voice narration to explain any astrophysics concept to middle/highschool students (and the general public) interested in space and astronomy. The video would also be interactive, pausing at certain sections for timed scenario-based questions to reinforce the concepts taught throughout the video.

I decided to focus on a vector animation style, detailing the explanation of gravity in different levels of difficulty, as a passion project. The project will consist of:

  • Background Research: Ensuring the information is accurate and appropriate for the audience's level.
  • User Research: Interviews/surveys with users to better understand the audiences who will see it, and to help guide the design and structure of the final product to better meet their needs.
  • Scripting and Storyboarding: Outlining the video, scripting the narration, storyboarding each section.
  • Design Development: Use Adobe Illustrator to build assets that can be used in each section, practice skills in After Effects, begin animating the final video.
  • Testing & Final Production Review sections with advisors/peers, combine with narration to complete the video.

Audience

  1. High School/Middle School students interested in physics/astronomy.
  2. Teachers/Professors who want a instructional aid.
  3. JMU Astronomy/Physics Department and General JMU Students
  4. General Public
